About the Game

The wild does not care how confident you are.

Bush IQ turns survival education into a playable training loop. Each module teaches a real principle, then tests whether the player can apply it under pressure.

Resources are limited, weather shifts, injuries matter, and poor planning follows you into the next day. The point is not to look tough. The point is to think clearly when comfort disappears.

Gameplay System

Every choice costs daylight.

Bush IQ drops players into a survival map where each location unlocks a field module. Study the principle, choose your action, manage your resources, and survive long enough to make it to extraction.

01

Study the Skill

Each module begins with a short survival lesson: water safety, shelter, fire, navigation, first aid, foraging, weather, or signaling.

02

Choose Your Action

Players decide where to spend time, energy, tools, and daylight. Safer choices may cost more resources. Faster choices may create bigger risks.

03

Survive the Consequence

Hunger, thirst, injuries, shelter quality, weather, and knowledge shift after every decision. Good judgment keeps you alive. Guessing gets expensive.

World Map Modules

Unlock the map by proving the skill.

Each hotspot opens a playable module. Completing one location can unlock the next survival zone, new gear, or harder conditions.

Base Camp

Open

Orientation and survival priorities.

Learn the first rule: shelter, water, fire, food, and signal. The order matters.

River Crossing

Open

Water sourcing and purification.

Decide whether to filter, boil, treat, or move on. Each option changes thirst, energy, and health.

Dense Woods

Open

Foraging and plant identification.

Identify useful plants, avoid harmful lookalikes, and decide whether the calories are worth the risk.

Ridge Shelter

Locked

Shelter site selection and weather defense.

Unlock this zone after completing Base Camp and River Crossing.

Signal Hill

Locked

Navigation, signaling, and route planning.

Use landmarks, sun position, map logic, and emergency signaling to move toward extraction.

Storm Zone

Locked

Weather reading and risk response.

Bad weather punishes weak shelter, poor timing, and overconfidence. It is very fair, which is to say, completely unforgiving.

Medic Point

Locked

First aid and injury management.

Treat cuts, dehydration, exposure, sprains, burns, and sickness before they compound.

Extraction

Final

The 30-day survival test.

Reach the final zone with enough health, knowledge, and supplies to complete the mission.
